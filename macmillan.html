Kickoff workshop planning and activities

The future of digital support

Whilst working with Macmillan to figure out the future of support for people living with cancer (PLWC), service design agency Humanly asked me to help out with the digital offering. Whilst they developed their real-world solutions, I was challenged with digesting a year's worth of research and finding how an app could help PLWC.

Concept & validation

During the research and ideation phase, I was developing potential ideas when one ended up striking a chord with the team and stakeholders. Whilst internally everybody was happy with the direction, I wanted to validate the idea alongside other concepts to ensure we were taking the right route. To do this we developed four 'smoke and mirrors' prototypes and ran a series of in-person testing sessions.

What I shared that got everyone excited

Final prototype

Whilst testing validated the preferred direction, by being able to compare the feedback against multiple concepts, we were able to garner greater insight into how to move forward with some of the finer details. With the direction decided, we set out to define a set of user stories and develop out the prototype. As we had already been using HTML prototypes, and as visual design wasn't a priority, it made sense to continue to build on what we had.
